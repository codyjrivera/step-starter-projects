Biography

I was born in California, although I have lived in many places across the United States, ranging from Colorado to Alabama, and now Nebraska. For high school, I attended Enterprise High School in Enterprise, Alabama; I graduated in spring 2018. I am currently attending the University of Alabama, majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics. I am doing undergraduate research as part of the Randall Research Scholars Program, an honors interdisciplinary undergraduate research program. (Fun Fact: Nan Boden, the COO of Robotics Moonshot at X, is an alumna of this same program). Currently, I am interested in an academic computer science career, focusing on parallel and distributed systems and theoretical computer science.

I started programming at age 15, when I took a robotics course at my high school. I soon joined my school's VEX robotics team. Through my participation in robotics, I discovered that I hate mechanical engineering, and I thus narrowed down my major choices to computer engineering or computer science. I further narrowed my choice down to computer science when I realized that computer science was not merely about software development; it is a broader way of thinking about the world. I became particularly interested in an academic career upon both doing research in high performance computing and reading more about theoretical computer science on my own time. I also added a major in Mathematics to give me a better foundation for the mathematical aspects of these areas of computer science. However, I have decided to do Google's STEP internship this summer (summer 2020) to round off my computer science experience.

In addition to my academic and professional work, I am also involved in several organizations across my university. I am currently the head of competitive programming -- we attended the ACM Southeast Programming Competition last fall. I am also involved in social events for my university's ACM chapter, as well as the Randall Research Scholars Program. Finally, I am a representative for Men Against Rape and Sexual Assault, a peer education organization that educates students in male-dominated campus organizations about sexual assault prevention and bystander intervention.
